Biomaterials and Biodesign
Waste to Resource

Waste to Resource: Green Cotton Muslin Regeneration is a collaboration between the Fashion Institute of Technology and Stony Brook University. This research project focused on taking cotton muslin from a scrap bin at the Fashion Institute of Technology and using green chemistry to test how the textile can be broken down into usable fibers. The end goal was to then resynthesize these fibers into new cotton; promoting a more sustainable future of cotton production. I was responsible for conducting initial tests for this research and experimenting with different variables and conditions. Additionally, I was responsible for recording results and analyzing data for accompanying conclusions and predictions.
